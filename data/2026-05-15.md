### [1] [Taming Wild Knots with Mosaics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14185)
*Mary Y. Deng,Allison K. Henrich,Sean H. Kawano,Andrew R. Tawfeek*

Main category: math.GT

TL;DR: 本文提出了一种新的方法来研究具有孤立奇异点的野结（wild knots），通过将Lomonaco和Kauffman的纽结镶嵌理论扩展到无限根树结构，并引入了镶嵌缠结和镶嵌刚性顶点空间图的概念。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 野结（具有无限打结行为的结）因传统分类方法失效而长期被视为拓扑学中的奇闻异事，缺乏系统研究。作者希望建立一种新的表示框架，使这类对象能被有效分析。

Method: 将Lomonaco和Kauffman的纽结镶嵌理论扩展到无限情形，用无限根树表示野结，树的顶点赋予镶嵌，连接通过嵌入函数控制。同时引入了镶嵌缠结和镶嵌刚性顶点空间图（镶嵌奇异结为其特例）作为拓展框架。

Result: 成功为具有孤立奇异点的一类野结子类建立了基于无限根树的镶嵌表示方法，并构建了相关的扩展概念体系。

Conclusion: 该框架为野结的分类和研究提供了新的可行路径，将野结从拓扑学的边缘话题推进为可系统研究的对象领域。

Abstract: Wild knots--knots with infinite knotting behavior--have resisted traditional methods of knot classification, making them more of a curiosity in topology than a subject of sustained investigation. In this paper, we present a new way to investigate these objects. We extend Lomonaco and Kauffman's knot mosaic theory to represent a substantial subclass of wild knots that have isolated wild points. Our mosaics consist of infinite rooted trees with mosaics assigned to vertices and embedding functions governing connections. In developing this framework, we also introduce a notion of mosaic tangles as well as mosaic rigid vertex spatial graphs of which mosaic singular knots are a special case.

### [3] [Geodesic currents of coarse negative curvature](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14469)
*Meenakshy Jyothis,Dídac Martínez-Granado*

Main category: math.GT

TL;DR: 本文证明了在曲面上的测地流空间中，具有强双曲性的子集是稠密的，但由非正曲率度量诱导的测地流并不稠密，并由此构造了无穷多非粗略等距的强双曲度量。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 强双曲性是一种比Gromov双曲性更强的粗曲率概念，它包含所有CAT(-k)度量（k>0），并能利用负曲率中的动力学工具（如热力学形式）。本文旨在研究测地流空间中强双曲性的分布及其与负曲率度量的关系。

Method: 利用一个初等有限覆盖论证，结合强双曲性在测地流对偶伪度量边界数据上的刻画，证明了强双曲性在测地流空间中的稠密性；并通过构造反例说明非正曲率度量对应的测地流不满足稠密性。

Result: (1) 强双曲测地流在测地流空间中稠密；(2) 非正曲率度量诱导的测地流不稠密；(3) 构造了无穷多互不粗略等距的强双曲度量，它们不是CAT(0)的；(4) 建立了相关长度谱的关联计数结果。

Conclusion: 强双曲性在测地流空间中具有普遍性（稠密），但非正曲率度量却非常特殊（不稠密），这揭示了强双曲性与非正曲率在曲面上的本质差异。

Abstract: Strong hyperbolicity is a coarse notion of negative curvature, stronger than Gromov hyperbolicity, that includes all CAT(-k) metrics for k positive and allows the use of dynamical techniques available in negative curvature, such as thermodynamical formalism. We prove that the subset of geodesic currents whose dual pseudometric is strongly hyperbolic is dense in the space of geodesic currents. The proof combines an elementary finite-cover argument with a characterization of strong hyperbolicity in terms of boundary data for pseudometrics dual to geodesic currents. In contrast, we show that currents arising from non-positively curved metrics on the surface are not dense. As a consequence, we construct infinitely many pairwise non-roughly-isometric invariant strongly hyperbolic geodesic metrics on the universal cover of the surface which are not CAT(0). Finally, we establish correlation counting results for the associated length spectra.
