Despite their utility, the fast computation of persistence diagrams of sliding window embeddings is still poorly understood. ### [3] [Discrimination of Dynamic Data via Curvature Sets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04624)
*Nadezhda Belova,Maxwell Goldberg,Facundo Memoli,Sriram Raghunath,Andrew Xie*

Main category: math.AT

TL;DR: 本文提出动态曲率集持久同调方法，用于分析动态数据，通过证明其具有反链可分解性，并开发高效算法计算侵蚀距离，实现了对动态行为的有效区分。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统拓扑数据分析方法在处理动态数据时，虽然能有效更新持久图，但难以区分在固定时间等距但定性不同的行为。Kim和Mémoli提出的时空持久框架产生了多参数持久模块，但计算上存在挑战。

Method: 将Gómez和Mémoli的曲率集概念扩展到动态设置，提出动态曲率集持久同调方法。证明所得多参数持久模块具有反链可分解性，并开发高效算法计算侵蚀距离。

Result: 动态曲率集持久同调方法产生的模块是区间可分解的（实际上是更强的反链可分解）。算法能高效计算任意反链可分解模块间的侵蚀距离，且构造对广义Gromov-Hausdorff距离稳定。在Boids模型实验中成功检测到参数变化。

Conclusion: 动态曲率集持久同调为分析动态数据提供了计算高效且理论稳定的框架，能够有效区分看似相似但本质不同的动态行为，在动物群集行为等应用中具有实用价值。

Abstract: Techniques from topological data analysis (TDA) have proven effective in studying time-dependent data arising in dynamic systems, such as animal swarming behavior and spatiotemporal patterns in neuroscience. While early algorithms leveraged efficient updates to persistence diagrams for dynamic data, they struggled to distinguish behaviors that are isometric at each fixed time but differ qualitatively. This limitation was addressed by Kim and Mémoli, who introduced a spatiotemporal persistence framework for dynamic metric spaces, resulting in multiparameter persistence modules. However, these modules pose computational challenges.
  To address this, we build on insights from Gómez and Mémoli, who observed that the homology of Rips complexes over size $(2k+2)$ point subsets of a metric space--termed principal curvature sets--is both tractable and informative. We extend this idea to dynamic settings by introducing dynamic curvature-set persistent homology, applying the spatiotemporal framework of Kim and Mémoli to curvature sets. We prove that the resulting multiparameter persistence modules are interval-decomposable: in fact, they possess a stronger property we term antichain-decomposable. Utilizing this property, we present a new algorithm to efficiently compute the erosion distance $d_E$ (due to Patel) between arbitrary antichain-decomposable modules (including, but not limited to modules produced by our construction). Additionally, our construction is stable with respect to a generalized Gromov-Hausdorff distance between time-dependent datasets proposed by Kim and Mémoli. This enables a robust computational pipeline for distinguishing dynamic data, as demonstrated in experiments with the Boids model, where we successfully detect parameter changes.

### [4] [The Minkowski problem of $p$-affine dual curvature measures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05144)
*Youjiang Lin,Yuchi Wu*

Main category: math.MG

TL;DR: 论文构造了凸体的p-仿射对偶曲率测度族，研究了其Minkowski问题，给出了偶Minkowski问题解存在的充分条件，以及p∈(0,1)时的必要条件。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究凸体几何中的p-仿射对偶曲率测度，建立其与现有测度（如仿射不变测度和经典锥体积测度）的联系，并探讨相关的Minkowski问题。

Method: 构造了p-仿射对偶曲率测度族，证明其极限情况与已知测度的关系，提出并研究相应的Minkowski问题，包括给出偶Minkowski问题解存在的充分条件和p∈(0,1)时的必要条件。

Result: 成功构造了p-仿射对偶曲率测度，建立了其与仿射不变测度和锥体积测度的极限关系，给出了偶Minkowski问题解存在的充分条件，以及p∈(0,1)时的必要条件。

Conclusion: p-仿射对偶曲率测度是凸体几何中重要的新测度族，其Minkowski问题的研究为相关偏微分方程理论提供了新的研究方向。

Abstract: For $p\in (-\infty,0)\cup(0,1)$ and a convex body $K\subset\mathbb{R}^n$ with the origin in its interior, we construct the family of $p$-affine dual curvature measures $\mathcal{I}_p(K,\cdot)$ with respect to $K$. The affine-invariant measure $\widetilde{C}_{n-1}^{\mathrm{a}}(K, \cdot)$ given in the paper [9] is the limit case of $\mathcal{I}_p(K,\cdot)$ as $p\rightarrow 1^-$. The classical cone-volume measure is the limit case of the affine measures $2|p|\mathcal{I}_p(K,\cdot)/(n^2V(\mathrm{I}_pK))$ when $p\rightarrow 0$ and $V(K)=2$, where $\mathrm{I}_pK$ denotes the $L_p$ intersection body of $K$. The Minkowski problems for the $p$-affine dual curvature measures are proposed and studied. Specifically, we give a sufficient condition for the existence of a solution to the even Minkowski problem for $p$-affine dual curvature measure. Moreover, a necessary condition is given when $p\in (0,1)$. The smooth case of this Minkowski problem is equivalent to solving a new type of partial differential equations with respect to $p$-cosine transforms.
