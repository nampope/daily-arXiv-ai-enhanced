The first is a point-vector dimension, which records exact local directions. The second is a point-tangential dimension, which replaces exact directions by Bouligand effective directions. The third is the Point-Cross Dimension, which weights these effective projective channels by the point-extended box complexity detected along admissible probes. We establish the basic structural properties of these invariants and compute the resulting Point-Cross Dimension on a range of model configurations, including finite crosses, fractal coordinate frames, oscillatory germs, self-similar curves, Sierpiński-type carpets, Cantor dusts, and infinite-rank outlook examples. The final part of the paper establishes comparison principles between the directional and dispersive layers of the theory.
