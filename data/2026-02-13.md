*Michal Jablonowski*

<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 为特殊交错纽结图寻找数值不变量，该不变量应能反映图的组合结构和交叉符号信息，并在flype变换下保持不变。

Method: 利用特殊交错图的Tait图的拉普拉斯矩阵及其Moore-Penrose伪逆，构造一个二次迹表达式作为不变量。该表达式在flype变换下保持不变，且可用加权图作为电路网络的总有效电阻来解释。

Result: 定义了不变量FP，证明其在flype变换下不变。通过计算flype相关图对验证了不变性，并提供了若干素交错纽结的FP值。

Conclusion: 成功构造了一个新的纽结数值不变量FP，该不变量基于拉普拉斯矩阵，在flype变换下保持不变，为纽结分类和研究提供了新工具。

Abstract: We introduce a new numerical invariant for special, reduced, alternating diagrams of oriented knots and links, defined in terms of the Laplacian matrix of the associated Tait graph. For a special alternating diagram, the Laplacian encodes both the combinatorics of the checkerboard graph and the crossing signs. While its spectrum depends on the chosen diagram, we show that a specific quadratic trace expression involving the Laplacian and its Moore-Penrose pseudoinverse is invariant under flype moves. The invariant admits an interpretation in terms of total effective resistance of the associated weighted graph viewed as an electrical network. Explicit computations for pairs of flype-related diagrams demonstrate that, although the Laplacian characteristic polynomials differ, the invariant FP coincides. Values for several prime alternating knots are provided.

### [4] [Universal Ahlfors--David regularity of Steiner trees](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11294)
*Danila Cherkashin,Pavel Pozorov,Yana Teplitskaya*

Main category: math.MG

TL;DR: 该论文给出了Steiner树问题的定量正则性结果，证明了在几乎每个ε>0时，Steiner树除去ε邻域后的集合是Ahlfors-David正则的，且常数仅依赖于维度d。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Steiner树问题是寻找最小长度的集合使得给定紧集连通。Paolini和Stepanov已经提供了存在性和正则性的一般结果，但缺乏定量估计。本文旨在给出不依赖于给定紧集A的定量正则性结果。

Method: 通过分析Steiner树的结构性质，证明对于d>2的情况，Steiner树除去ε邻域后的集合满足Ahlfors-David正则性条件，即Hausdorff测度在球内的增长有上界估计。

Result: 证明了对于d>2，Steiner树除去ε邻域后的集合是Ahlfors-David正则的，常数仅依赖于维度d。作为推论得到了密度型结果：该集合在球内最多包含特定数量的线段。

Conclusion: 本文给出了Steiner树问题的定量正则性结果，将先前的一般正则性结果具体化为不依赖于给定紧集的定量估计，特别在平面情况下可以得到更紧的结构结果。

Abstract: The celebrated Steiner tree problem is the problem of finding a set $\St$ of minimum one-dimensional Hausdorff measure $\H$ (length) such that $\St \cup \mathcal{A}$ is connected, where $\mathcal{A} \subset \mathbb{R}^d$ is a given compact set.
  Paolini and Stepanov provided very general existence and regularity results for the Steiner problem.
  Their main regularity result is that under a natural assumption, $\H(\St) < \infty$, for almost every $\varepsilon>0$ the set $\St_\varepsilon := \St\setminus B_\varepsilon(\mathcal A)$ is an embedded finite forest (acyclic graph).
  We give a quantitative regularity result by proving that the set $\St_\varepsilon$ is Ahlfors--David regular with constants that depend only on $d$ (and not on $\mathcal{A}$). Namely, for $d > 2$, every $\varepsilon > 0$, every $x \in \St_\varepsilon$, and every choice of $ρ\in (0,1)$, we have
  \[
  \frac{\H(\St_\varepsilon \cap B_{ρ\varepsilon}(x))}{\varepsilon} \leq \left ( \frac{64d}{1-ρ} \right) ^{d-2}.
  \]
  As a corollary, we obtain a density-type result, i.e. that the set $\St_\varepsilon \cap B_{ρ\varepsilon}(x)$ consists of at most
  \[
  \left ( \frac{64d}{1-ρ} \right) ^{d-1}
  \]
  line segments.
  In the plane (i.e., for $d=2$), it is possible to obtain tight structural results.

</details>


### [5] [Metric geodesic covers of graphs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11657)
*Jerry Chen,Kyle Hess,Matthew Romney*

Main category: math.MG

TL;DR: 研究一维拓扑空间的最小测地线覆盖问题，通过计算机辅助找到最优覆盖，确定了一些标准图的覆盖数，并给出了覆盖数为3的空间分类。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究一维拓扑空间的最小测地线覆盖问题，即寻找用测地线覆盖空间的最小数量。这个问题涉及组合几何和拓扑的交叉领域，对于理解空间的结构性质有重要意义。

Method: 提出了约简方法，使得能够通过计算机辅助找到图的最优测地线覆盖。使用这些方法确定了几个标准图的覆盖数，包括K4、K5和K3,3。还构建了覆盖数为3的拓扑空间目录。

Result: 确定了K4、K5和K3,3等标准图的测地线覆盖数。构建了覆盖数为3的拓扑空间完整目录，并由此推导出任何覆盖数为3的空间必须是平面的。

Conclusion: 通过计算机辅助方法成功解决了测地线覆盖问题，建立了覆盖数与平面性之间的联系，为拓扑空间的结构分析提供了新工具。

Abstract: We study the problem of finding, for a given one-dimensional topological space $X$, a cover of $X$ of smallest size by geodesics with respect to some metric. The infimal size of such a set is called the metric geodesic cover number of $X$. We prove reductions enabling us to find, with computer assistance, optimal geodesic covers of a graph and use these to determine the cover number of several standard graphs, including $K_4$, $K_5$ and $K_{3,3}$. We also give a catalogue of topological spaces with cover number $3$, and use it to deduce that any such space must be planar.
