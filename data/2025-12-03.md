### [2] [On Topology of Three-dimensional Continua with Singular Points](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02385)

TL;DR: 提出用Yin集（有界边界的正则开半解析集）来建模三维连续体的拓扑结构，将奇异点作为核心研究对象而非异常，并给出基于粘合曲面的唯一边界表示方法。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统基于流形的模型将奇异点（切线平面不唯一的边界点）视为异常，但实际三维连续体中这些点是普遍存在的。需要一种能系统处理奇异点的拓扑模型，以更好地应用于固体建模、CAD和多相流拓扑变化模拟等领域。

Method: 定义Yin集作为建模工具，研究其局部和全局拓扑性质。提出基于粘合曲面（可定向紧致2-流形沿一维CW复形的商空间）的唯一边界表示方法。

Result: 建立了Yin集的拓扑特征理论，给出了适用于任意复杂拓扑三维连续体的边界表示方法。该方法能系统处理奇异点，为复杂拓扑结构提供了统一的数学框架。

Conclusion: Yin集模型为三维连续体拓扑提供了比传统流形模型更全面的理论框架，特别适合处理奇异点。该方法在固体建模、CAD和多相流模拟等应用中具有实用价值。

Our model differs from manifold-based models in that singular points of a 3D continuum, i.e., boundary points where the tangent plane is not uniquely defined, are treated not as anomalies but as a central subject of our theoretical investigation. We characterize the local and global topology of Yin sets. Then we give a unique boundary representation of Yin sets based on the notion of a glued surface, a quotient space of an orientable compact 2-manifold along a one-dimensional CW complex. Our results apply to 3D continua with arbitrarily complex topology and may be useful in a number of scientific and engineering applications such as solid modeling, computer-aided design, and numerical simulations of multiphase flows with topological changes.

Method: 引入局部Gromov扭曲概念，建立当两个闭连通子集的局部扭曲低于维度相关常数g_n且Hausdorff距离小于尺度四分之一时，其补空间基本群同构的判据。将此应用于一维扭结图像，推导扭结等价性的稳定性条件。

In addition, we construct this isomorphism so that it restricts to the corresponding peripheral subgroups as an isomorphism as well.

From that we deduce novel stability results for knot equivalence in the Lipschitz category, and in the setting of fractional Sobolev regularity below $C^1$. Moreover, we prove a compactness theorem of knot equivalence classes with respect to weak $W^{3/2,2}$-convergence.
  As an application we show that the Möbius energy introduced by O'Hara~\cite{ohara_1991a} can be minimized within arbitrary prime knot classes under a symmetry constraint, and that these minimizers are in fact critical points and therefore smooth and even real analytic. In particular, in every torus knot class there are at least two distinct critical knots for the Möbius energy.
