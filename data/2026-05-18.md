### [1] [On Pappus and Anosov Representations of the Modular Group](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15317)
*Richard Evan Schwartz*

Main category: math.GT

TL;DR: 本文研究模群到对称空间X=SL_3(R)/SO(3)的等距群中的离散忠实表示空间DFR，证明其有一个同胚于R²×[0,∞)的Barbot分支B，边界参数化Pappus表示，内部为Anosov表示。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 理解模群在秩2对称空间中的离散忠实表示空间的结构，特别是Barbot分支的几何与拓扑性质。

Method: 通过对模群到Isom(X)的表示进行分类，关注将2阶生成元映到唯一不动点的等距的表示，构造并证明DFR中存在一个同胚于R²×[0,∞)的分支B。

Result: 证明了DFR有一个Barbot分支B，该分支同胚于R²×[0,∞)；其边界对应Pappus表示，内部对应Anosov表示。

Conclusion: 本文完整刻画了Barbot分支的拓扑结构，并揭示了Pappus表示与Anosov表示在该分支中的位置关系。

Abstract: Let $X=SL_3(\R)/SO(3)$. Let $\cal DFR$ be the space of discrete faithful representations of the modular group into ${\rm Isom\/}(X)$ which map the order $2$ generator to an isometry with a unique fixed point. In this paper, we prove that $\cal DFR$ has a component $\cal B$, the so-called Barbot component, that is homeomorphic to $\R^2 \times [0,\infty)$. The boundary of $\cal B$ parametrizes the Pappus representations and the interior consists of Anosov representations.

### [2] [Detecting Causality with the Links--Gould Polynomial](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15515)
*Vladimir Chernov,Matthew Harper,Ben-Michael Kohli*

Main category: math.GT

TL;DR: 本文研究时空因果性与光射线链环不变量的关系，发现Links-Gould多项式能区分所有已知的Allen-Swenberg反例链环，从而检测因果性；它还继承了经典Alexander-Conway多项式的特征，但比后者更强。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Low与Natario-Tod猜想以及Penrose问题提出：时空因果性是否可以用光射线流形中球面链环的缠绕性质来表述？Chernov-Nemirovski在(2+1)维时空中肯定解决了这一问题，进而引出哪些链环不变量可用于研究因果性的问题。Allen-Swenberg进一步猜想Jones多项式（Khovanov同调的交替欧拉示性数）足够，同时构造了反例链环AS(n)表明Alexander-Conway多项式（Heegaard-Floer同调的欧拉示性数）不够。因此需要寻找更强的不变量。

Method: 本文研究Links-Gould多项式——一种量子不变量，它能以两种不同方式退化为经典Alexander-Conway多项式，并继承其部分经典特征。作者将此多项式应用于Allen-Swenberg构造的所有反例链环，检验其能否区分因果无关事件对应的链环。

Result: Links-Gould多项式成功区分了所有Allen-Swenberg链环与因果无关事件对应的链环，从而在已知所有Alexander-Conway多项式不足的例子中检测出因果性。此外，作为推论，作者计算了所有Allen-Swenberg链环的Seifert亏格。

Conclusion: Links-Gould多项式可能完全捕捉时空因果性，其能力显著强于Alexander-Conway多项式。值得注意的是，该多项式的范畴化（即构造其同调理论）是一个困难且尚未解决的问题，不在本文讨论范围之内。

Abstract: The conjectures of Low and Natario--Tod, and Penrose's question on Arnold's Problem list ask if causality in spacetimes can be formulated in terms of linking of spheres of light rays in the manifold of all light rays. For $(2+1)$-dimensional spacetimes, this link happens in the manifold coverable by a solid torus $S^1\times \mathbb R^2$. This was solved positively by Chernov and Nemirovski, which raises the question of which link invariants can be used to study causality. Chernov, Martin and Petkova proved that Heegaard--Floer and Khovanov homology completely capture causality.
  Allen--Swenberg conjectured that the Jones polynomial, which is obtained as an alternating Euler characteristic from Khovanov homology, is also sufficient. But they constructed complicated examples of links $\mathrm{AS}(n)_{n=1}^{\infty}$ that suggest that the Alexander--Conway polynomial -- which is the Euler characteristic of Heegaard--Floer homology -- is not enough.
  The Links--Gould polynomial is a quantum invariant that specializes to the classical Alexander--Conway polynomial in two different ways and somewhat surprisingly inherits some of its characteristic classical features. We show that it distinguishes all the Allen-Swenberg links from the link of causally unrelated events and hence detects causality in all known examples where the Alexander--Conway polynomial is not sufficient. This suggests that it may completely capture causality. The work on the categorification of the Links--Gould Polynomial is an ongoing and hard problem, and it is not a subject of this paper.
  As a corollary, we also compute the Seifert genus of all Allen--Swenberg links.

  <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 通过扩展阿波罗尼斯圆的传统定义，使其在欧氏几何中曲线不变，但在双曲和球面几何中更便于处理；并分析该圆与两圆等光曲线之间的关系。

Abstract: We extend the old definition of the Apollonius circle in such a way that it results in the same curve in Euclidean geometry but will be more convenient in hyperbolic and spherical geometries. We show that there exists an Apollonius circle of the centers of two circles that coincides with their equioptic curves, as in Euclidean geometry.

### [5] [Area of Hölder curves and coarea formula on the Heisenberg group](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15987)
*Gioacchino Antonelli,Robert Young*

Main category: math.MG

TL;DR: 本文证明了从海森堡群 $\mathbb H_n$ 到 $\mathbb R^{2n}$ 的Lipschitz映射的余面积公式，通过引入新的积分定义$\frac12$-Hölder曲线的辛面积并利用$\beta$-数估计克服纤维不可求长的问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 解决Magnani、Kozhevnikov等人遗留的余面积公式在子黎曼几何中向量值情形的问题。

Method: 引入新积分定义$\frac12$-Hölder曲线及垂直曲线投影的辛面积，给出该积分收敛的几何条件，并利用Fässler–Orponen Dorronsoro定理的$\beta$-数估计证明该条件对几乎所有纤维成立。

Result: 成功证明了从$\mathbb H_n$到$\mathbb R^{2n}$的Lipschitz映射的余面积公式，并得到了$\frac12$-Hölder平面曲线有符号面积存在性的新结果。

Conclusion: 该工作填补了子黎曼几何中余面积公式向量值情形的空白，提出的方法对不可求长曲线的几何测度论有独立价值。

Abstract: We prove the coarea formula for Lipschitz maps from the subriemannian $n$th Heisenberg group $\mathbb H_n$ to $\mathbb R^{2n}$. Our result is new even when $n=1$ and provides the simplest vector-valued instance of the coarea formula in subriemannian geometry. This answers a question left open in the works of Magnani, Kozhevnikov, Magnani--Stepanov--Trevisan, and Julia--Nicolussi Golo--Vittone.
  The main difficulty of the proof is that a fiber of a $C^1_{\mathrm{H}}$ map $f: \mathbb H_n\to \mathbb R^{2n}$ is typically an unrectifiable curve. Its measure depends on the symplectic area of its projection to $\mathbb R^{2n}$. A bound on this area would imply the coarea formula, but examples of Kozhevnikov show that this area can be infinite or undefined.
  To overcome this, we introduce an integral that we use to define both the symplectic area of $\frac{1}{2}$--Hölder curves in $\mathbb R^{2n}$ and the symplectic area of projections of vertical curves in $\mathbb H_n$. Then, we give a geometric condition for this integral to converge. This yields, in addition, new results on the existence of the signed area of $\tfrac12$--Hölder planar curves that may be of independent interest. Finally, we use $β$--number estimates from the Fässler--Orponen Dorronsoro Theorem to show that this geometric condition holds for almost every fiber.

</details>
